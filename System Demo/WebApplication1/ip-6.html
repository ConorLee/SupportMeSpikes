User Declaration

Please ensure that the service user carefully reads and understands the following:

The Welcome Organisation works to support those who are homeless or at risk of homelessness. If your needs do not fall within one of these categories our service may not be the best service to meet your needs. If this is the case a member of staff will signpost you to a more appropriate service.

You can only access our services if you are aged over 18. If you are under 18 you will not be permitted access to The Welcome Organisation. This includes visitors to our service. If you are under 18 and in need of support, staff will refer you to a more appropriate service.

You cannot access Drop-In services if you have a conviction which falls within the categories of sexual or schedule one offending. If you are unsure of what a sexual or schedule one offence is a staff member can explain this in more detail.

Service rules

If you are suitable for our service we would ask that you follow the rules below:

  • Do not use illegal substances in the project
  • Do not bring illegal substances into the project
  • Do not give, exchange or sell medication to another person
  • Do not accept or purchase medication from another person - Medication includes medication prescribed by a GP and medication that you can purchase from a chemist>
  • Do not consume alcohol in the project - If you bring alcohol into the project this must be passed to staff when you first enter the project
  • Do not harass, abuse or assault anyone in the project
  • Do not steal anything in or from the project, and do not bring stolen goods into the project
  • Do not bring offensive weapons to the project
  • Do not cause material damage to the project - This includes Welcome Organisation property and the property of those visiting, working in or using our service
